Problem solving

Tell us about a time that you felt blocked on a simple problem? There have been a lot of times I have been stuck on problems during most of my time in foundations where I have over thought the questions. This is all a new area of learning for me so learning the new words and breaking things down and simplifying them have really helped! A problem I encountered during the kata exercises was assigning properties and defining them as an object. The way I solved this problem was reaching out for help on discord and one of the facilitators pointed me in the right direction which reminded me what the answer was. Often I need a bit of guidance to give me a clue as to what I am doing.

The question was "The value of the terah children property should be defined as an object" When I first read that question my mind automatically gets confused as to what my first step should be Straight away I over thought the question and started to write a new variable named terah which didn't seem to work at all because the object terah had already been declared earlier in the exercise. This is where my overthinking gets the better of me.

The solution was to just use 

terah.children: {}
and then insert the children between the {} It was an ah ha! Moment for me where I realised not to over analyse questions too much as the answer they are wanting could be something completely simple!

A Time I elegantly solved a problem When I was trying to figure out how to manipulate objects at the start I couldn't remember the syntax of how to set up an object with it's properties I went over my notes and watched the javascript cafe video which I knew had the answer in it as I remembered doing the same thing to set up the coffee products and it gave me the guidance I needed to start it off I felt very accomplished for having figured it out without asking for help. and I learnt that sometimes the answers are right in front of me I just have to look for them

Some of the things that have helped me to problem solve include

  • Pseudocode
  • Trying something/Trouble shooting
  • Reading error messages
  • console.logging
  • Googling/Youtube
  • Asking my peers/Facilitators for help
  • Doing some self reflections
