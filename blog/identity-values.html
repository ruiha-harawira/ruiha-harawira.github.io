Identity, values and ethics

When I think of my values I think of respect, compassion, loyalty and whanau. I hold a lot of gratitude towards my upbringing as the saying goes “It takes a village to raise a child” and I really did have a village of strong-minded, driven and loving Whānau members supporting me and helping my mother to raise me. Growing up my Koro was my father figure in my life, a man of many talents. An ex SAS and vietnam war veteran, a teacher, a curator for museums along with many other things. From a young age he instilled the saying that “knowledge is power” always encouraging me to read books and continuously up skill wherever I can.

Along with my Koro there was also my mother. She is such a selfless and generous person. I watched her work her butt off when I was younger to provide for us and save up enough money to get our first home. My mother is one of the strongest woman I know, a true mana wahine in my eyes and one of my biggest role models. She has taught me a lot about hard work and resilience but also how to be kind and caring.

I also think my culture is a huge part of my identity I pride myself on being Maori and I know that I can be successful in the modern world but also pupuri ki aku tikanga (hold on to our cultural customs and practices), and be humble.

I think one of my biggest strengths is resilience and being able to get back up after feeling defeated. Something that I will be working on during my time at dev academy will be my self confidence. I tend to feel stressed when I feel something is not up to the standard I would like it to be. I also want to get out of the habit of comparing my work to my class mates. I just need to believe in myself more.
