Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people. Understanding neuroplasticity is very beneficial as it gives people the knowledge that your brain is elastic it can grow, adapt and change and it is a way to condition your brain to have better experiences. Every time you learn something new you are harnessing the power of neuroplasticity.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit. To enhance neuroplasticity in my life I will be practicing the principle of repetition. To help generate changes in my brain I need to keep practicing and working on something so that It becomes like second nature to me, and I can constantly improve on something if I know I am comfortable with what I am doing

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity? Some of the ways to increase neuroplasticity are by doing repetition or following the use it or lose it method which in other words means if you stop performing a task or learning something for a long period of time the circuits in your brain will eventually forget how to do it or be a bit rusty because it hasn't been required for that specific task. And this is why it is so important to continuously practice things or you will eventually forget how to do it.

Growth mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant. A growth mindset is having the ability to be open minded to learn and acknowledge that you are capable of doing things. The only thing stopping you, is yourself. If you continuously live in a fixed mindset and tell yourself "I'm not good at math or I'm not good at running" you are essentially telling your brain these things and it will start to believe it. But if you have a growth mindset and tell yourself I might not be good at this but I can teach myself or I am willing to learn you will always be in the mindset of growing and being the best version of yourself which in turn can actually be extremely beneficial for your mental health and your growth in every aspect of your life.
